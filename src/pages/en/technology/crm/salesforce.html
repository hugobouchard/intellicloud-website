Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a Salesforce implementation take?

Essential setup: 6-10 weeks. Advanced implementation: 12-16 weeks. Enterprise ecosystem: 20-26 weeks. Timeline depends on data migration complexity, number of integrations, custom development needs, and user training requirements.

Do I need Sales Cloud, Service Cloud, or both?

Sales Cloud for managing opportunities, forecasting, and sales pipeline. Service Cloud for support tickets, knowledge base, and case management. Most companies start with Sales Cloud, then add Service Cloud as support volume grows. We assess your needs during discovery.

What's the difference between Experience Cloud and a website?

Experience Cloud is a branded portal connected to your Salesforce data. Customers log in to check order status, pay invoices, view service history, and submit tickets—without calling support. It's not a marketing website; it's a self-service customer portal.

Can you migrate data from our old CRM?

Yes. We migrate accounts, contacts, opportunities, activities, and custom data from any CRM (Microsoft Dynamics, Zoho, SugarCRM, spreadsheets, etc.). We clean data, deduplicate records, map fields, and validate accuracy before go-live.

Will you train our team on Salesforce?

Absolutely. We provide role-based training: admin training for your Salesforce administrator, user training for sales reps and support agents, and executive training for dashboard interpretation. Includes recorded sessions and documentation.

Do you provide ongoing Salesforce support?

Yes. After implementation, we offer managed services retainers ($3K-$10K/month) for ongoing configuration changes, user support, new integrations, quarterly health checks, and Salesforce release updates (3 per year).

Can Salesforce integrate with our other systems?

Yes. We integrate Salesforce with marketing automation (HubSpot, Marketo), accounting (QuickBooks, NetSuite), ERP systems, custom databases, email platforms, and 5,000+ apps via Salesforce AppExchange. Most integrations use native connectors or REST APIs.
