CASE STUDY: B2B SaaS Company

A B2B SaaS company with 25-person sales team had implemented Salesforce themselves 18 months prior. Adoption was 34%, data quality was terrible (duplicate accounts, inconsistent data entry), and executives didn't trust pipeline reports. Sales ops spent 15 hours per week cleaning data manually. The VP of Sales couldn't forecast accurately and was making hiring decisions blind.

We reimplemented Salesforce properly: mapped their entire sales process and configured custom stages, objects, and fields to match; automated lead routing by geography and company size; integrated with HubSpot for marketing attribution and Intercom for support ticket visibility; built executive dashboards showing real-time pipeline health and forecast accuracy; created automated workflows for quote generation and approval; trained the team with hands-on workshops using real deals. Made it easy to use correctly and hard to use wrong.

Results in 90 days: Adoption increased from 34% to 91%, sales cycle shortened from 67 days to 39 days (42% faster), forecast accuracy improved to 94% (executives trust the numbers), sales ops time on manual cleanup dropped from 15 hours/week to 2 hours/week, pipeline visibility increased 67%, win rate up 18% (better qualification data). Total investment: $42,000. Added annual revenue from faster sales cycles and better forecasting: $2.4M.

Why do most Salesforce implementations fail to deliver ROI?

Most Salesforce implementations fail because they're configured as generic CRMs that don't match the actual sales process. Without proper customization, integration, and change management, Salesforce becomes an expensive database that nobody trusts. Successful implementations require understanding your specific sales process and configuring Salesforce to support it—not forcing your team to adapt to out-of-box settings.
