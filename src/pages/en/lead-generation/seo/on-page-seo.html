Frequently Asked Questions

What is on-page SEO and why does it matter?

On-page SEO is the practice of optimizing individual web pages to rank higher in search results and earn more relevant traffic. It matters because it's one of the few ranking factors you have complete control over. Properly optimized pages signal relevance to Google and provide clear value to visitors, resulting in better rankings and higher conversion rates. Our clients see an average 34% ranking improvement within 90 days.

How is on-page SEO different from technical SEO?

On-page SEO focuses on content-level optimization—titles, headers, content quality, keyword usage, and schema markup on individual pages. Technical SEO focuses on site-wide infrastructure—site speed, crawlability, mobile optimization, and indexing. Both are essential, but on-page SEO is where you signal topical relevance and create conversion opportunities. We optimize both as part of our comprehensive SEO strategy.

What's included in your on-page SEO service?

Our on-page SEO service includes: (1) Title tag and meta description optimization for click-through rate and rankings, (2) Header tag structure and hierarchy (H1-H6) optimization, (3) Content optimization with strategic keyword placement and semantic relevance, (4) Internal linking strategy to distribute page authority, (5) Image optimization with descriptive alt text, and (6) Schema markup implementation for rich snippets. Every element engineered for both search engines and human visitors.

How long does it take to see results from on-page SEO?

Initial ranking improvements typically appear within 2-4 weeks as Google recrawls optimized pages. Significant ranking gains usually occur within 60-90 days. Conversion rate improvements from better-optimized content often happen faster—within 1-2 weeks of implementation. Timeline depends on competition level, site authority, and current optimization state. We provide weekly ranking reports so you can track progress in real-time.

Can you optimize existing pages or only new content?

We optimize both existing and new pages. In fact, optimizing existing pages often delivers the fastest ROI because you're improving assets that already have some authority and traffic. We conduct a comprehensive content audit, identify quick-win optimization opportunities, and prioritize pages by potential impact. Many clients see ranking improvements within weeks just from optimizing their existing high-traffic pages.

How do you measure on-page SEO success?

We track four key metrics: (1) Keyword rankings—position changes for target keywords, (2) Organic traffic—sessions from search engines to optimized pages, (3) Click-through rate (CTR)—percentage of searchers who click your result, and (4) Conversion rate—percentage of organic visitors who take desired actions. You receive a dashboard showing all metrics with before/after comparisons. Our average client achieves 34% ranking improvement, 41% CTR lift, and 23% conversion rate increase.
