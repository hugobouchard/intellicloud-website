Frequently Asked Questions

What is local SEO and how is it different from regular SEO?

Local SEO optimizes your online presence to attract customers from specific geographic areas. It focuses on appearing in the Google Map Pack (the 3 local business listings that appear with a map at the top of search results), near me searches, and location-specific organic rankings. Different from traditional SEO which targets broad keyword rankings, local SEO emphasizes proximity signals, local citations, reviews, and Google Business Profile optimization. 46% of all Google searches have local intent, making local SEO essential for businesses serving specific geographic markets.

Complete local search domination.

Significant map pack positions (top 3) usually occur within 60-90 days depending on competition. Near me search visibility often improves within 4-6 weeks. Review generation and citation building show cumulative benefits over time. Our clients see an average 87% increase in local leads and 78% increase in foot traffic within 90 days.

Do I need local SEO if I already have a Google Business Profile?

Yes—Google Business Profile is one component of local SEO, but there's much more. Local SEO includes citation building across 50+ directories, NAP consistency management, location-specific content creation, local link building, and strategic optimization across all local ranking factors. Most businesses have a profile but lack the comprehensive optimization needed to actually rank in the map pack. It's like having a website but no SEO—you exist but nobody finds you.

What are local citations and why do they matter?

Local citations are mentions of your business name, address, and phone number (NAP) on other websites—directories like Yelp, Yellow Pages, industry-specific sites, and local business listings. They matter because Google uses citations to verify your business legitimacy and location. Consistent citations across 50+ directories signal trust and authority. Inconsistent citations (different phone numbers, old addresses) confuse Google and hurt rankings. We audit, correct, and build citations systematically to maximize local search authority.

Can you handle local SEO for multiple locations?

Absolutely—multi-location local SEO is one of our specialties. Each location needs individual optimization: separate Google Business Profiles, unique citations, location-specific content pages, individual review strategies, and market-specific keyword targeting. We prevent location cannibalization (locations competing against each other) while maximizing each location's visibility in its local market. Our multi-location clients dominate map pack results across all their service areas.
